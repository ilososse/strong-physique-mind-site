    Programs & Progress Journal

    This page presents a key tool that makes a difference for motivation: automated tracking of each session you perform.
    As an athlete, I long dreamed of having such a tool available: now it exists — the hack is here, your turn to use it!

    The idea is to:

    • if needed, choose a level at the bottom of this page that fits you (or try several),
    • select or enter the exercises you are performing — the AI tool allows you to record your exercise by counting reps and/or hold time!
    • document your progress and feelings across sessions with the great tool I provide below,
    • appreciate your progress over time through the charts automatically generated from your sessions.
    • ⚠️ it is extremely important to warm up your muscles before starting exercises.
    • I invite you to read my article on warm-ups, which presents an example of exercises to prepare for physical effort.

      ℹ️ Programs by level

      Further down this page you'll find three broad level categories – Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced / Pro – with example workout structures. There are obviously endless ways to combine exercises. My proposal is a starting point. Feel free to adapt it to your level by choosing exercises of varying difficulty!

      Beginner

      Simple full-body routine

      • 3 × 8–10 incline push-ups (hands on floor / couch)
      • 3 × 10 bodyweight squats
      • 3 × 20 s plank hold
      • 3 × 5–10 negative pull-ups

      Goal: learn the basics, feel the movements, build initial consistency without overloading the nervous system.

      Intermediate

      Pull / Push routine

      • 4 × 6–8 tractions strict pull-ups
      • 4 × 8–12 classic push-ups
      • 3 × 8 dips on bars or chairs
      • 4 × 10 s handstand hold (wall or freestanding if comfortable)
      • 4 × 3–10 s tuck planche holds
      • 4 × 3–7 handstand push-ups if you're persistent

      Goal: consolidate base strength and prepare the ground for skills (muscle-up, front lever, more advanced planche variations).

      Advanced / Pro

      Skills & Explosiveness Session

      • Muscle-up: 5–8 sets of variations (explosive, assisted, negatives)
      • Front lever: 5 × 10–15 s on your current progression (tuck, advanced tuck, straddle…)
      • Handstand push-ups: 4 × 3–12 (wall + freestanding)
      • Planche: 5 × 3–10 s on the progression that suits you best (tuck, straddle or full if you feel monstrous)

      Goal: develop skills, muscle, and ogre-like strength.
